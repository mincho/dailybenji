# Daily Benji — Feature Catalog

*Source-of-truth for marketing copy and imagery. Generated 2026-05-24. Keep this in sync as features ship.*

## The Pitch (one paragraph)

Daily Benji is a calm, privacy-first way to actually track what you spend — built around a single morning email that summarizes yesterday, surfaces patterns, and flags what's coming next. You feed it transactions by forwarding bank alerts (no bank logins), typing them in, or uploading a CSV. It does the categorizing, the math, and the noticing. You get one tidy briefing a day. That's it. **$5/month flat, no upsells, cancel anytime — and when you cancel, everything you ever put in gets deleted.**

## Why Daily Benji exists

Every feature in this catalog is in service of a small set of real, *felt* problems. The marketing site should lead with these — not with a feature list. Nobody signs up for "smart auto-categorization." They sign up because something in their relationship with money is bothering them. This section names those somethings.

### The problems we're really solving

**"I make decent money. I'm not in debt. And I still have no idea where it goes."**
This is the most common situation by a wide margin. It isn't a crisis — it's the quiet, ongoing unease of looking at a bank balance and feeling disconnected from your own life. Full budgeting apps feel like overkill for it; doing nothing leaves the unease in place. Daily Benji is the middle path: awareness without homework.

**"Mint shut down and everything that replaced it is worse."**
Intuit killed Mint at the start of 2024 and left millions of people scrambling. The replacements each fail differently: Monarch and Copilot want $15/month and your bank credentials. Rocket Money and Credit Karma push affiliate credit-card and loan offers. YNAB demands you build a zero-based budget. Daily Benji is the boring middle: doesn't ask for your bank login, doesn't sell anything, doesn't make you budget.

**"I don't want anyone — not even a 'trusted' aggregator — having my bank login."**
For a meaningful slice of people this is a deal-breaker, full stop. Engineers who know what an OAuth scope is. People who've been hacked before. People who read the Plaid class-action coverage and noped out. Bank alerts are the data source that lets these people participate in personal-finance software at all, because the alerts themselves don't contain credentials or account numbers.

**"My partner and I keep having the same money conversation."**
Couples argue about spending more than almost anything else, and usually not because anyone is reckless — because there's no shared, neutral baseline. One person feels surveilled when asked about a charge; the other feels in the dark when they aren't. A morning email both people read replaces "what did you buy at Target?" with no conversation at all, because everyone already knows.

**"Where is $200/month going to subscriptions I didn't notice?"**
Free trials from three years ago. Apps you stopped opening. Streaming services that quietly raised prices. The active-subscriptions roll-up and subscription-price-change observations are the answer — and they catch the ones you'd never remember to look for on your own.

**"I'd like to save more. I just can't see what's stopping me."**
You earn enough to save. You're not saving enough. The numbers don't add up and the apps don't explain it. The opportunity-cost projections ("Starbucks ≈ $760/year — compounded ≈ $10,510 over 10 years") aren't a guilt trip; they're the math no one had ever put in front of you.

**"Tax season is a nightmare and I keep putting it off."**
Freelancers, contractors, side-hustlers, anyone who deducts work-from-home expenses. The workflow — tag a transaction as Work Expense, paperclip the receipt, then export a ZIP of CSV + receipts filtered to that tag — is what makes April survivable. It replaces a shoebox.

**"I had a spreadsheet. I gave up on it in week three."**
Manual tracking dies because of friction. Email forwarding is the lowest-friction tracking method that exists — set up a rule once, then never think about data entry again. The spreadsheet wasn't the problem; the daily work of it was.

**"My life just changed and the old autopilot doesn't fit."**
New baby. New house. New job. Divorce. A parent moving in. A layoff. Whatever ran on autopilot before doesn't now. People in transition need a clear look at what's actually happening before the next decision. A daily email is exactly the right amount of presence — enough to learn the new shape, not so much that it becomes a project.

**"I get fifty bank alerts a day and I've started ignoring all of them."**
The alerts are the right *data* in the wrong *format*. Daily Benji takes the same firehose and turns it into one synthesized morning briefing. Same information, one moment of attention instead of fifty.

**"I want to know without being lectured."**
A lot of finance software assumes you need to be coached, gamified, or scolded into better behavior. Some people just want a mirror, not a personal trainer. Daily Benji reports; it doesn't moralize.

### Who this is for

Not "anyone with money." The people who will actually love this share something:

- **They want to understand their spending, not optimize it.** No leaderboards, badges, or streaks. The goal is awareness, not gamification.
- **They prefer reading to clicking.** A morning email beats opening an app, every time.
- **They've been burned by — or never trusted — bank-aggregator apps.** Plaid is a hard "no."
- **They don't want a budget.** Or they tried YNAB and it didn't take. They want a mirror, not a coach.
- **They want it to end if they want it to end.** No retention dark patterns. No held-hostage data. Cancel, deletion, done.
- **They'll happily pay $5/month for software that isn't trying to sell them something else.**

A few concrete archetypes that fit:

- The 30- or 40-something professional who earns well, isn't in trouble, and is quietly bothered that they can't say where last month went.
- The Mint refugee who's been limping along on a spreadsheet since 2024 and finally wants to stop.
- The privacy-conscious tech worker, journalist, lawyer, or healthcare worker who has reasons — vague or specific — not to hand over bank credentials.
- The couple with merged finances who want a shared, low-conflict baseline.
- The freelancer who dreads tax season and wants a tagged, receipt-attached audit trail without buying QuickBooks.
- The recovering over-spender who's out of credit-card debt and wants ongoing awareness without a strict budget.
- The newly-married, newly-parented, newly-divorced, or newly-relocated person whose old money habits don't fit the new life.
- The retiree on a fixed income who wants confidence they're staying in their range, in a format their reading habits already include.

## Three Pillars

These are the differentiators. Lead with these on the homepage hero, sub-hero, and "why us" sections.

### 1. Your Daily Benji — One email, every morning

A real personal-finance briefing — not a notification, not a digest, not a sales pitch. Every morning at the hour you choose, in your time zone, you get one email that recaps yesterday's spending, places it in context, surfaces what's coming next, and rotates through deeper sections through the week. It's the only place you need to look. The dashboard is there if you want it; the email is enough if you don't.

### 2. Privacy-first by design

- **No bank connections.** No Plaid, no Yodlee, no aggregator. We never have your bank login.
- **Your data is your data.** Add, edit, delete anything. You're not asking permission.
- **No third-party access.** Your transactions don't leave our infrastructure.
- **No ads. No selling data. No upsells.** $5/month is the entire business model.
- **Real deletion.** Cancel and your full history — every transaction, every memo, every tag — is permanently removed. We show you the count before you click.

### 3. Three ways to add spending — whichever fits your life

We meet you where you are. Most people land on email forwarding once they see how little effort it is, but every method is first-class:

- **Bank-alert forwarding.** Set up forwarding rules for your bank's transaction alert emails to your unique Daily Benji address. We parse the amount, merchant, and last-4 of the card. Bank alerts don't contain account numbers or credentials, so you get automation without ever handing anyone the keys.
- **Manual entry.** Spreadsheet-style bulk add with autocomplete. Perfect for cash, splits, or anything your bank didn't email about.
- **CSV import.** Upload exports from your bank, credit card, or budgeting tool. We remember the column mapping for next time.

## Feature Catalog

Use this as a checklist for the website's feature sections. Each item is one bullet's worth of marketing copy.

### Adding Spending Data

- **Bank alert email forwarding** — works with 14+ major banks out of the box (Chase, Amex, Capital One, Citi, Bank of America, Discover, U.S. Bank, Wells Fargo, Barclays, Navy Federal, PNC, TD Bank, USAA, Synchrony) plus a generic profile for anything else.
- **Onboarding wizard with bank-specific setup guides** — step-by-step screenshots for the bank you actually use, plus Gmail / Outlook / Apple Mail / Yahoo forwarding setup.
- **Manual transaction entry** — a single-page bulk-add form with autocomplete on merchants, categories, and accounts. Add 20 transactions in 60 seconds.
- **CSV import** — most bank/card export formats. The app remembers the column mapping per source so subsequent imports are one-click.
- **Cash account, automatic** — every account is auto-provisioned with a "Cash" account for transactions that don't come from a bank.
- **Multi-currency support** — forwarded emails in EUR, GBP, CAD, AUD, JPY, CHF, MXN, NZD, SGD, HKD, INR, BRL, KRW, THB auto-convert to USD using daily exchange rates. The original amount + currency is preserved on every transaction.
- **Duplicate prevention** — we de-duplicate on the email's Message-ID so forwarding the same alert twice never creates a double-charge.

### Your Daily Briefing Email

- **One email, every morning, at the hour you pick** — choose your delivery time and time zone; we deliver in your local time, not ours.
- **Yesterday at a glance** — hero number, transaction count, comparison to your 30-day average.
- **Yesterday's transactions, grouped by card** — every charge with merchant, category, tag, and amount.
- **Anticipated spend** — recurring charges expected in the next 5 days, predicted from your own history.
- **Active goals progress** — up to 3 goals with pace and projection inline.
- **Discretionary vs essential breakdown** — instantly see how much of yesterday was the kind of spending you actually control.
- **Rotating weekly deep-dives** — a different lens every day so you're not getting the same email seven times a week:
  - **Monday**: Category breakdown with 7-day vs 30-day trend
  - **Tuesday**: "Who Gets Your Money?" — most-visited merchants, biggest one-offs, top 30-day recipients
  - **Wednesday**: Spending Observations — anomalies, subscriptions, opportunity-cost projections
  - **Thursday**: Discretionary spending focus with category-level trends
  - **Friday**: Account overview — spending split by card
  - **Saturday**: Goal coaching — on-pace, over-pace, and where to adjust
  - **Sunday**: Full weekly review with year-to-date totals
- **Additional recipients** — optionally CC a spouse, partner, or accountant.
- **Reply to ask questions** — see *Conversational Replies* below.

### Tracking & Organizing

- **Categories** — system categories cover common spending (Groceries, Restaurants, Coffee/Snacks & Treats, Travel, Utilities, Subscriptions, etc.).
- **Smart auto-categorization** — every transaction is auto-categorized using a three-tier system: your personal overrides → community-shared merchant rules → an AI fallback (Claude Haiku) that learns the long-tail. Once classified, a merchant is remembered forever.
- **Tags** — your own labels with custom colors. Apply to a single transaction or a whole merchant. Used everywhere: filters, exports, the daily email's Sunday section.
- **Memos** — free-text notes on any transaction. Surfaced in the transaction list, detail page, and CSV export.
- **Receipt attachments** — attach photos or PDFs of receipts to any transaction. Paste a screenshot with Cmd+V, drag and drop, or pick from your computer — all three work. JPG, PNG, HEIC, WEBP, or PDF; up to 5 per transaction; 100 MB of storage included per account. A paperclip icon shows in the transactions list wherever a receipt is attached.
- **Recurrence marking** — mark a charge as monthly, annual, or one-time. The app auto-applies the pattern to other charges from the same merchant at similar amounts.
- **Non-spending markers** — three layers to exclude things from "spending":
  - Per-transaction (one specific charge isn't spending)
  - Per-merchant (every charge from this place isn't spending — e.g. transfers to yourself)
  - Per-category (system-managed — Transfers, Credit Card Payments, Reimbursements, Investments, Business Expense are excluded by default)
- **Soft delete + restore** — deleting a transaction is reversible until you really want it gone.
- **Reminders on transactions** — set a reminder on any charge ("follow up on this refund by Tuesday"). Due reminders surface in the daily email and dashboard.

### Multiple Ways to See Your Money

- **Dashboard** — hero card with this month's spend, daily average, yesterday, today, projected month-end. A 30-day sparkline. Spending observations preview. Active goals. Due reminders.
- **All Activity** — searchable, filterable transaction table. Filter by date range, account, category, tag, search by merchant/memo/amount. Inline category and recurrence editing. Bulk update.
- **By Category** — every category with totals, percentages, and discretionary flags. Drill into any category's transactions.
- **By Recipient** — every merchant, grouped, with lifetime spend, last activity, average charge.
- **By Tag** — same shape for your custom tags.
- **By Account** — top-level "Accounts" page with one card per account: lifetime transaction count, last-30-day spending, last 3 transactions, and an inline rename. Active vs Dormant split based on 90-day activity.
- **Calendar view** — month heatmap of your spending days. Click any day to see what happened.
- **Anticipated Spend** — full list of predicted recurring charges with confidence based on history depth.
- **Spending Insights** — a flat feed of dated, verifiable observations the system has noticed. See next section.

### Insights Worth Reading

Daily Benji's Insights surface specific, dated patterns. Each observation cites real transactions so you can verify it instantly. Dismiss any you don't want to see again.

- **Anomalies** — "Starbucks: $42 on May 8 — about 6× the typical $7 from this merchant."
- **Category swings** — "Restaurants is up 54% in the last 30 days ($420 vs $273 the prior 30)."
- **Largest day this month** — the single biggest day, with its top 3 charges.
- **Spending pace** — "You're 28% behind the prior 3-month average at this point in May."
- **Day-of-week patterns** — "62% of your Restaurants spend lands on Friday."
- **New merchants** — first-time charges from a place you've never paid before.
- **Subscription price changes** — Netflix went from $15.49 → $17.99.
- **Possibly canceled subscriptions** — monthly charges that have gone silent for 45+ days.
- **Active subscriptions roll-up** — "11 active monthly subscriptions totaling $187/month — $2,244/year if all renew."
- **Opportunity-cost projections** — for any discretionary merchant you visit 20+ times in 90 days: "Starbucks ≈ $760/year — at a 10-year S&P 500 average at 7%, that compounds to ≈ $10,510."

### Goals

- **Spending goals** of four kinds:
  - **Total goal** — overall spending cap
  - **Recipient goal** — limit spending at a specific merchant
  - **Category goal** — cap a category (e.g. Restaurants under $400/month)
  - **Tag goal** — cap anything you've tagged
- **Time frames** — last 7 days, last 30 days, this calendar month, or a custom window.
- **Goal coaching in the daily email** — Saturday's section walks through every active goal with on-pace / over-pace status, projected outcome, days remaining, and where to ease up.
- **Auto-name generation** — goals get sensible default names you can override.

### Account Management

- **Linked accounts** — when you get a new card with a new number for the same real account, link the two together. Transactions stay on whichever card was used, but every total, chart, and email rolls them up as one. Reversible at any time.
- **Account merge** — destructive sibling of link: collapse two accounts into one when they really are duplicates (e.g. one created via email forwarding, one via CSV import).
- **Account nicknames** — rename "Chase ending 4729" to "Joint checking" and see it everywhere.
- **Inline rename** — change a nickname from the Accounts page without bouncing through settings.
- **Manage Accounts page** — nicknames, link, and merge live together under one Settings page.

### Sharing & Collaboration

- **Add a collaborator** — invite a spouse, partner, or accountant to share your account. They see everything you see, can add and edit transactions, and submit support requests. No separate magic email — forwarded transactions land in the same shared account regardless of who forwards them.
- **Demo mode** — anyone can try the app without signing up. A real account with real-looking data, rolling forward every night so it's always current. Read-only.

### Reminders & Conversational Replies

- **Set reminders on transactions** — "remind me about this refund on Tuesday." Surfaces in your daily email and dashboard until you mark it done.
- **Conversational replies to the daily email** — hit reply on your daily Benji email and ask a question in plain English. The app interprets natural-language requests against your data and replies. Useful for "What did I spend at restaurants last month?" or "Show me everything at Whole Foods this quarter."
- **In-app support** — every dashboard page has a Contact support link. Submit a message and the team replies by email; conversations are threaded inside the app for context.

### Search & Export

- **Search** — every page that lists transactions has a search box that hits merchant names, memos, and amounts.
- **CSV export** — full transaction list with date, recipient, account, amount, category, tags, memo, non-spending flag, original currency, original amount, and the filenames of any attached receipts.
- **ZIP export with receipts** — filter transactions however you like (by tag, date range, category, account) and download the matching set as a ZIP: the CSV plus a folder of every receipt file attached to those transactions. Great for tax season — filter to "Work Expense" or "Reimbursable" and hand the whole bundle to your accountant.
- **Copy to clipboard** — quick share single transactions for chat or email.

### Mobile-Ready

- **Mobile-friendly web app** — every page is built mobile-first; no separate app to install, no app store gatekeeping.
- **Side nav adapts** — hamburger on small screens, expanded sidebar on desktop.
- **Touch-friendly bulk edit + swipe-scroll tables** — dense admin/data tables scroll horizontally instead of being squished or clipped.

### Privacy & Data Ownership

- **No bank logins. Ever.** We don't connect to your bank. We don't ask for your banking credentials. We don't use Plaid, Yodlee, MX, or any aggregator. Bank alert emails are the only data source — and bank alerts don't contain account numbers or credentials by design.
- **Your data is yours.** Add, edit, delete anything, anytime.
- **No ads. No third-party trackers. No data brokers.** $5/month subscription is the entire business model.
- **Real deletion** — when you delete your account, every transaction, tag, memo, goal, observation, support request, and email log is permanently destroyed. We show you the exact count of what'll be removed before you confirm.
- **Encrypted at rest, transit-secured.** Standard hosting security; we don't take shortcuts.
- **US-only customer base** — we don't sell to data brokers anywhere, and our defaults (date format, USD) match where our customers actually live.
- **Forward emails get auto-deleted on a 30-day rolling window** — we don't hoard the raw inbound mail; only the parsed transaction stays.

### Pricing

- **$5 per month, flat.** That's it.
- **No upsells, no premium tier, no usage-based add-ons.** Every feature in this document is included.
- **Cancel anytime.** Self-serve from your settings. No "talk to sales," no "are you sure" arms race.
- **Cancel and we delete everything.** Your full transaction history, every memo, every tag — permanently removed. No "we'll keep a backup."

## Tone & Voice Notes

- **Calm, not breathless.** Never "supercharge", "unlock", "transform your finances".
- **Specific, not vague.** "11 active subscriptions totaling $187/month" beats "see your subscriptions".
- **One number, no asterisks.** $5/month is a feature, not a price.
- **Privacy as conviction, not compliance.** "No bank logins. Ever." reads stronger than "we take your privacy seriously."
- **Avoid hustle language.** This is for people who want to *understand* their spending, not 10x it.

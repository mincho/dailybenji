Privacy

No bank logins. Ever.

Daily Benji runs on the alerts your bank already sends you — which, by design, contain nothing sensitive. Here's exactly what that means.

A bank alert is built to be read on your lock screen. There's nothing in it a thief could use.

Transaction alerts are designed to land in your inbox and flash on your phone, so banks deliberately leave out anything dangerous: no full account number, no full card number, no balance, no login. They carry the merchant, the amount, the date, and maybe the last four digits of the card — and that's all Daily Benji ever sees.

That's the whole trick. You get real automation by forwarding something that was already safe to sit in your inbox — without ever handing anyone the keys to your accounts.

What we see — and what we don't

We only ever receive the alert emails you choose to forward. Nothing else.

What we see

  • The merchant name
  • The amount
  • The date
  • Your bank's card name or nickname
  • Sometimes the last few digits of the card

What we never see

  • No bank login or password
  • No full account or card numbers
  • No account balances
  • No Social Security or license numbers
  • Nothing that needs a bank connection

How we keep it that way

No bank connections

No third-party apps or account connections of any kind. We never have — and never ask for — your bank login.

No ads, no data brokers

The $5/month subscription is the entire business model. We don't sell or share your data, full stop.

Payments handled by Stripe

Card details go straight to Stripe over an encrypted connection. We never see or store your full card number.

Forwarded emails don't linger

Once we've parsed an alert into a transaction, the original email is deleted automatically. We don't hoard your inbox.

Encrypted in transit and at rest

Every connection is secured, and your data is encrypted where it's stored. Access is tightly limited.

Real deletion

Cancel and your full history — every transaction, memo, and tag — is permanently erased. We show you the count before you confirm.

The fine print, in full

Everything above in proper detail — what we collect, the providers we use, your rights, and data deletion timelines.
